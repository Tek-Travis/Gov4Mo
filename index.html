Candidate Profile

About Travis John Richard

Born in St. Martinville, Louisiana, and raised in Cotter, Arkansas, and Gainesville, Missouri, Travis John Richard has called Springfield home for the past 20 years. He is committed to practical, people-first reforms that strengthen Missouri’s working families, farms, and communities.

Top 5 Priorities

The Working Families Tax Fairness Act provides essential relief by establishing a permanent income tax exemption for Missouri’s bottom 50%. This policy eliminates "zero-liability" earners' tax filings, funding the gap by closing loopholes and limiting capital gains for the ultra-wealthy. It prioritizes household stability over regressive consumption taxes, ensuring families keep more of their hard-earned money.
Eliminating state and local sales taxes on groceries and fuel provides immediate, essential relief for Missouri families. By treating these necessities as non-taxable, we end regressive policies that disproportionately burden those living paycheck-to-paycheck. We will fund this common-sense reform by closing corporate tax loopholes and protecting vital community services through dedicated infrastructure funding.
This fair tax policy establishes a 10% rate for the Top 1% and billionaires, alongside a 15% corporate tax rate, to fund essential public services. By exempting the bottom 50% from income taxes and providing relief to the middle class, we shift the fiscal burden onto those most capable of contributing, effectively lifting working families out of poverty.
To build a thriving Missouri, this plan establishes a 10% tax rate for the Top 1% and billionaires, alongside a 15% corporate rate. By exempting the bottom 50% from income taxes and providing targeted middle-class relief, we shift the fiscal burden to those best able to contribute, effectively lifting working families out of poverty..
The Missouri Home Stability & Independence Fund grows our middle class by investing directly in homeowners. Through grants for solar installations, well repairs, and energy-efficiency upgrades, we slash monthly living costs for families. Combined with first-time homebuyer assistance, this policy builds lasting wealth, promotes self-reliance, and strengthens the foundation of every Missouri community.
The program provides full medical coverage with no deductibles or co-pays for the bottom 50% of income earners, eliminating the "poverty trap" where individuals avoid necessary preventative care due to cost.
Unlike traditional plans, Medicaid for All mandates the inclusion of vision, dental, hearing, and mental health services—including the provision of essential hardware like eyeglasses, hearing aids, and therapy—as standard covered benefits, along with full coverage for depression treatment.
To ensure long-term viability, the top 10% of income earners contribute via an income-related co-pay structure. This ensures that those with the highest ability to pay support the system that sustains a healthy, productive workforce for the entire state.
The system is financed through a stable blend of federal healthcare grants, state budget allocations, and a dedicated 15% corporate contribution rate. This shifts the financial burden away from household premiums and onto the entities that benefit most from a healthy, stable economy.
By folding existing private insurance overhead and fragmented billing systems into the unified Medicaid for All administration, the state will achieve massive cost savings. These funds are redirected entirely to patient care, doctor compensation, and frontline medical services.
I will make it illegal for investors and large corporate landlords to buy and hold single-family homes. Homes should belong to families, not Wall Street. Corporate ownership drives up prices and reduces opportunity, so my policy protects local buyers and keeps neighborhoods built around people, not speculation.
I will create low-interest home loans backed by state treasury bonds to help more Missourians buy homes. These loans will make homeownership more reachable for working families, first-time buyers, and people who have been priced out of the market. Building equity is one of the strongest ways to grow the middle class.
I support rent limits for low-income renters, people with disabilities, and seniors over 65. No one living on a fixed income should be forced out of their home because rent keeps rising faster than their paycheck or benefits. This policy gives vulnerable Missourians stability and protects them from displacement.
I will modernize Missouri’s power grid with geothermal energy and solar farms. A stronger grid means more reliable power, lower pollution, and better long-term energy security. By investing in clean energy now, we can reduce costs for families while creating a more sustainable future for the entire state.
I will expand grants for water wells and solar system installations, especially for rural and low-income homeowners. I will also extend energy-saving programs that help families reduce monthly utility bills. These investments lower long-term costs, improve independence, and make homes more affordable to live in year-round.
Missouri should move major government services into one secure digital portal so residents can handle tasks faster, with less paperwork and fewer delays. This includes voter registration, vehicle tags, boat and RV registrations, license renewals, and other common transactions. A modern system saves time, reduces waste, and makes government easier for everyone to use.
Missouri education should be fully online and fully accredited so students across the state can learn from home or on their own schedule. A strong online system gives families more flexibility while keeping teacher support, testing, and graduation standards in place. This creates a modern education model that works for rural and urban communities alike.
Libraries should be expanded into learning centers across Missouri with high-speed internet, computers, and hands-on educational classes. These centers can support students, job seekers, and adults who need help with technology or schoolwork. They should become community hubs for learning, training, and public access.
Missourians should be able to earn their GED online and move directly into better opportunities. I will offer two years of free college and two years of free trade school to build a stronger workforce. We need more plumbers, carpenters, electricians, and HVAC workers to meet the needs of Missouri families and businesses.
Missouri should remove wasteful school boards and outdated systems that take money and time away from students. The goal is a simpler, more direct education structure that focuses on learning instead of bureaucracy. Resources should go to classrooms, students, and teachers, not extra layers of administration.
Missouri should create a 7-day emergency fund to help families keep rent paid and lights on during a crisis. If a household loses income, suffers an injury, or faces the death of a spouse or loved one, OCAC support should cover rent and electric for up to 90 days to prevent deeper poverty.
Lifting families out of poverty means no one is left behind. Homeless shelters should be turned into true temporary stays, with a direct path into safe and secure housing, including group homes, rentals, or permanent homes. The goal is not just shelter, but stability, dignity, and a real chance to get back on their feet.
To keep people from falling into poverty, Missouri must expand mental health services, rural hospitals, and local clinics. Too many families are pushed into crisis because they cannot get care close to home. Stronger health access means earlier treatment, fewer emergencies, and better long-term stability for working people.
Missouri needs stronger disaster relief funds and grants to help families and communities recover after tornadoes, floods, or fires. This should include tornado shelter funding and relocation help for families living in flood zones. No family should lose everything and be left without a safe place to restart.
The best way to fight poverty is to prevent it before it starts. Missouri should combine emergency aid, housing support, and disaster recovery with long-term stability programs that help families stay secure. When we protect people during their hardest moments, we build stronger communities and a safer future for everyone.
